ℹ What do the severity levels mean?
▼
⛔ Blocking

A conformant record in the source standard cannot be made fully conformant in the target standard without either discarding a mandatory field or fabricating a value that was never present.

The receiver may still be able to parse the file, but it will fail validation against the target standard. This is the most severe level and typically means a round-trip is not safe without human review.

⚠ Lossy

Conversion is possible and the record will pass target validation, but some information is permanently degraded in the process.

Example: a structured PointOfContact (name, role, email as separate sub-fields) maps to a free-text creator field — the values transfer but the internal structure is flattened and cannot be recovered on the return trip.

↻ Transform required

The data is fully interoperable, but only after a specific, well-defined transformation is applied. No information is permanently lost.

Examples: mapping a NATO security classification vocabulary to Dublin Core rights statements; converting ISO 8601 dates to a different date format; splitting a combined field into two separate fields. The transformation must be explicitly defined and consistently applied.
